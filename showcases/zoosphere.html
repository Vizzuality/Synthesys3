About the Project

Specimen postal loans can be lengthy, inherently risky and are not always possible. A solution to this is to provide digital ‘surrogates’ of specimens that can be accessed and usefully studied. Developed by the Museum fur Naturkunde Berlin, Zoosphere is a digitisation tool that aims to generate an open, international repository for high-resolution, multi-angle image sequences of biological specimens. As well as facilitating and speeding up the research process, this also improves the accessibility of scientific specimens by making them available to the general public.

How it works

The ‘ZooSphere’ itself consists of a turning frame upon which a pinned specimen is set, in front of a camera which is mounted on a movable slide. Once calibrated, the ZooSphere software instructs the frame and turntable to move in particular ways, allowing image capture from up to 1,000 different perspectives around the object. The images are then stacked and tiled, and the resulting sequence is published on the ZooSphere website .

Zoosphere has been featured on the Google Cultural Institute .

Using ZooSphere

The specimens currently available on ZooSphere can be freely browsed and searched for. The project itself remains in an early stage, with wide release of both hardware and software components still pending. Please see ZooSphere website for updates or follow the project on Social Media.
