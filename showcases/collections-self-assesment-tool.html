Collections Self-Assessment Tool

At a glance

Creating a unified and accessible assessment methodology for care of Natural History collections.

About the Project

The Collections Self-Assessment tool (CSAT) is a free online tool, developed during SYNTHESYS2 (2008 – 2013), as a means of allowing institutions to assess aspects of their collections care and management with an objective and unified set of criteria.

How it works

Each institution that signs up to the CSAT can register as many departments as required, each of which is assessed separately under each of the following categories:

  • Collections Management
  • Collections Care
  • Collections Access
  • Molecular Collections
  • Digital Collections

The infrastructure and buildings standards of the institution as a whole are also assessed.

There is currently a total of 89 distinct criteria across the 7 categories in the CSAT. The assessor will decide whether the institution has Not Met, Partially Met, Mainly Met, Met, or Exceeded each criteria, with a space to add clarifying comments. The resulting report uses a Red-Amber-Green traffic light system to demonstrate the urgency with which any unmet criteria should be addressed, along with advice on how best to approach doing so.

Self-assessments can be re-taken as many times as required by the institution to measure progress.

So far, 26 institutions have filled out the CSAT with 31 further institutions registered on the site or with assessments in progress. A 2015 update resulted in the inclusion of Digital and Molecular collections as part of the initiative to develop unified management policies for new physical and virtual collections.

To sign your institution up to the CSAT, please visit Synthesys and follow the link to log in or register your institution.
