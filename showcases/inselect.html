Inselect

At a glance

Inselect facilitates digitization by automating the capture of individual specimens from whole-drawer scans.

About the Project

Whilst imaging a whole drawer of specimens is relatively quick, manually segmenting the specimens is a time consuming process. Inselect was developed to address this problem – it can crop individual images of specimens from whole-drawer scans and similar images that are generated through digitisation of museum collections.

Inselect combines image processing, barcode reading, validation of user-defined metadata and batch processing to offer a high level of automation that can greatly increase the efficiency of digitisation workflows.

Once the draw is scanned and processed through Inselect, a segmented image is provided which gives the user the option to resize, move, create or delete segmentation areas. The metadata for each specimen can then be manually modified and entered by the user.

Development

Inselect was developed by a team at the Natural History Museum, in collaboration with scientists from Stellenbosch University in South Africa. A workshop was held in September 2014 to develop a specification and prototype, which was then presented at the Taxonomic Database Working Group (TDWG) meeting in October 2014. The program was subsequently developed for wider release and testing amongst SYNTHESYS3 Taxonomic Access Facilities (TAFs) and has undergone continuous development and improvement based on feedback from user communities.

To date, Inselect has been downloaded 737 times. It is cross-platform (Windows and Mac OSX) and open source with modular architecture. To download or read more about the software, including a full list of user guides, please visit the website or github.
